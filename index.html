    Voting for President

    Now it's time for the more well know part of US democracy out of the 3, voting for President. As most of you know, citizens of the US vote every 4 years for President, unlike both the US House of Representatives and Senate, which declare winners based on popular vote (how many people of a population voted for one specific candidate over the other). The US President is voted based on a system called the Electoral College. This system gives a state a certain number of points (electors) based on their state's population. This means that a big state like New York, with 16 million people currently living in it, won't have the same points as a small state like Wyoming, which only has 500 thousand people living there. In November, everyone votes in the "General Election," and people from each state vote on who they want to become President. Whoever has the most votes in each state would win that state, giving them that state's number of points. In the end, whoever gets the highest score (or passes 270 points) would win the election and become President of the United States.
